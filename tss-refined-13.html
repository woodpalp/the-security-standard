About The Security Standard
Defining the Future of Corporate Security

About The Security Standard.

The Security Standard is a practitioner-built platform that advances corporate security beyond traditional physical protection. We believe security should operate as a strategic, intelligence-driven function — measured, integrated, and aligned with the enterprise.

Most security programs measure activity.
Very few measure risk.
Almost none measure decision.

Security is no longer
a function of protection.
It is a function of decision.

From static controls to dynamic, decision-grade intelligence.

01 — The Context
The Shift

Security must evolve.

The risk environment has changed — and most security programs haven't kept pace.

Organizations face threats that span workplace violence, executive exposure, geopolitical instability, and operational disruption simultaneously. These risks don't respect functional silos — and security programs organized around silos can't manage them.

Fragmented, reactive security is a liability.

Security that operates as an afterthought produces afterthought outcomes. It must function as a measurable, integrated capability — aligned with enterprise risk management, business continuity, and the decisions executives are responsible for making.

The Security Standard exists to define
what that evolution looks like.
The Platform's Central Thesis

Security must evolve from
protection to decision intelligence.

From static protection to dynamic, decision-grade intelligence.

02 — Purpose
Our Purpose

A modern framework
for corporate security.

Our purpose is to build the framework that organizations use to understand, measure, and act on security risk — with the same rigor they bring to financial and operational risk.

Security that only protects is underperforming. It should also inform, enable, and strengthen the enterprise. That is the gap we exist to close.

Strategic

04 — Methodology
A New Standard

From perception-based to
decision-grade intelligence.

Most security programs are built on the wrong foundations.

Static policies, fragmented systems, and subjective assessments characterize legacy programs. The Security Standard replaces that with structured methodologies, repeatable processes, and outcomes that can be measured and defended.

Security should be held to the same standard as every other enterprise function.

That means scoring risk with consistency, establishing governance with accountability, and building architectures that hold up at scale — not just in controlled environments.

01
Standardized risk scoring and prioritization — consistent frameworks that remove subjectivity and create a shared language for risk across the enterprise.
02
Data-informed decision frameworks — replacing intuition-driven judgments with structured analysis that holds up under scrutiny at the board level.
03

04
Scalable architectures for global operations — security programs designed to operate consistently across geographies, business units, and risk environments.

Risk Modeling &
EROR Framework Assessment

The Enterprise Risk-Oriented Review (EROR) is a proprietary diagnostic framework developed through two decades of enterprise security program evaluation. It assesses your organization's posture across six dimensions — strategic alignment, operational capability, intelligence integration, governance maturity, resilience depth, and resource efficiency.

The output is not a compliance checklist. It is a board-ready strategic document — translating your security posture into the language of enterprise risk, with ranked findings, benchmarked scores, and a 12-month remediation roadmap tied to business priorities.

Service 03

Executive Protection
Program Design & Review

Modern executive protection is not bodyguard service. It is an intelligence-driven discipline that integrates physical security, digital threat assessment, reputational risk monitoring, travel security, and residential security into a coherent, proportionate program.

Most organizations have either over-invested in physical presence with little intelligence capability, or under-invested across the board because EP has been treated as a personal expense rather than an organizational risk management function. This engagement corrects both.

Engagement Scope
  • Current EP program assessment and gap analysis
  • Threat profile development for protected individuals
  • Digital footprint and online threat monitoring review
  • Travel security protocol assessment and design
  • Residential and home security review where applicable
  • EP vendor assessment, selection support, and contract review
  • Program policy, procedure, and protocol documentation
  • Protected individual briefings and security awareness sessions
Engagement Model
Assessment + Advisory
Initial assessment (2–4 weeks) followed by optional ongoing advisory retainer for program management and oversight.
Confidentiality
All EP engagements are handled with strict confidentiality protocols. Information is shared on a need-to-know basis with named contacts only.

Service 04

Event Security &
Travel Risk Management

High-profile corporate events represent concentrated, predictable risk. They require advance planning, intelligence, vendor coordination, and contingency development that most organizations either skip entirely or delegate to vendors not equipped to think at the strategic level.

Travel risk management is the other side: ensuring executives and personnel in complex international environments have the protocols, intelligence, and support structures to operate safely without curtailing organizational mobility.

Event Security Includes
  • Pre-event threat and vulnerability assessment
  • Venue security review and site advance work
  • Vendor briefing, coordination, and oversight
  • Credentialing and access control design
  • Emergency response protocol development
  • Command and control structure for day-of operations
Travel Risk Includes
  • Country and city-level risk assessment and tiering
  • Pre-travel intelligence briefings for traveling executives
  • In-country support and emergency contact protocols
  • Travel security policy design and implementation
  • Duty of care documentation and compliance
Engagement Model
Project or Retainer
Single-event engagements or ongoing travel risk advisory retainer for organizations with frequent international operations.
Lead Time Required
Event security: minimum 6–8 weeks. Travel risk assessment: minimum 5 business days. Emergency engagements assessed case by case.
Event Types
Investor summits · Board retreats · Product launches · Executive off-sites · Annual meetings · Leadership conferences
